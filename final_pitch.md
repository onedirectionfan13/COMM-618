Project Proposal: Timing Sanctions, Timing Conflicts: How U.S. Treasury Designations Against Hezbollah Financiers Correlate with Regional Crises

My final project will analyze the temporal relationship between U.S. Treasury sanctions on Hezbollah-affiliated individuals and entities and major Middle Eastern conflicts from 2001-2026. Using OFAC's Specially Designated Nationals database, I will examine when Hezbollah financiers were sanctioned, under which executive orders, and how these enforcement actions correlate with regional escalations. The analysis will focus on three key variables: designation dates, associated executive orders, and contemporaneous conflicts (2006 Lebanon War, Syrian Civil War, Gaza conflicts, etc.). By mapping sanctions timing against conflict timelines, the investigation aims to determine whether Treasury enforcement follows a reactive pattern to violence or proactive disruption strategy. Data sources include OFAC's downloadable SDN list, executive order archives, and conflict databases from academic institutions. Visualizations will include timeline charts showing sanctions clusters around major conflicts and network analysis of designated entities by conflict period. The story will explore whether sanctions timing suggests crisis-driven enforcement or strategic financial warfare, ultimately questioning the effectiveness of reactive versus preventive counterterrorism finance policy.
