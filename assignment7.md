Chart: Personal remittances received (% of GDP), regional comparison — Lebanon, Yemen, Jordan, Morocco and Egypt, 1970–2023. Lebanon’s post‑2019 jump is visible against steadier levels in neighboring countries. Source: World Bank; chart by Kelly Doyle.  
Middle Eastern and North African immigrants in the United States numbered roughly 1.2 million in 2022, and the Lebanese diaspora extends far beyond the U.S., concentrated in Gulf states, Europe and the Americas. Those communities — friends, former neighbors, children and grandchildren — have become primary channels for cash and material aid. Across the MENA region, the largest recipients of diaspora remittances are, in order, Lebanon, Yemen, Jordan, Morocco and Egypt — with Lebanon receiving far more as a share of GDP than the others.
